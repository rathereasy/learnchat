Introducing a new approach to learning and reviewing
  1. Learn and review by competing against others
  2. Transform learning into a fun social game
  3. Help students be motivated & excited to learn

TappFirst introduces competition to learning and reviewing, a simple idea yet surprisingly not put into use in the classroom or even online. Competition drives people to perform better: nobody likes losing in a game or sport. At school, students are able to compete in sports but when it comes to other courses, learning and reviewing often becomes a lonely, tedious and boring task for students. With TappFirst, we want to make learning more fun, effective and social.

Social, fun and competitive learning
  1. Competition drives people
  2. Learning with others is more engaging
  3. Competing with peers boosts attention

With TappFirst, students can learn and review the material taught in class with their peers by competing on a set of questions. Famous TV shows such as Jeopardy employ this idea and the concept is so popular that similar shows have been airing all around the world. Because of the competitive aspect of TappFirst, even students who do not have any interest for the course material start wanting to study more in order to win at the game. Competition also introduces a social aspect to learning and reviewing, which helps in making learning more fun and engaging.

Create and edit any questions on any subject
  1. Create questions on any subject
  2. Choose the number of questions and time limit
  3. Use your questions to make your students compete

As a teacher, you can use TappFirst to test your students on any amount of questions and any subject you want by simply signing up, creating a room and filling in your questions. Then, let your students know which room number corresponds to the set of questions you have made and the game begins. With TappFirst, there is no limit to the amount and variety of questions you want to test your students on. We let you the teachers decide how to use TappFirst, as we believe no software can replace teachers since teachers are irreplaceable. However, we do believe software can serve as a useful tool in learning.

Unlock your student's potential and lead them on the road to success
  1. Competitive learning increases retention
  2. Competition pushes students to learn faster
  3. Learning is more effective and fun!

Competition in learning and reviewing introduces a sense of urgency that pushes students to know the material as best as they can since there is no telling when the other peer will pick the right answer first. This introduces a bigger incentive to study and prepare for the quizzes in order to win. Furthermore, it pushes students to retain and know the material more deeply since the sense of urgency pushes users to pick the answer they think is correct as fast as possible without having much time to think. With TappFirst, the goal is not only to make learning more fun and exciting, but also to help students learn faster and more efficiently, ultimately helping them succeed in their learning.
